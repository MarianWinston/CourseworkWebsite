Literature Review

Mitigating corporate information exposure on the web

Introduction

According to Tayouri (2015), humans are social beings with a strong need to communicate and have relationships, it’s all part of the human nature. People always lived in groups and sometimes communicated around a fire, city markets, coffee’ shops and pubs. In the modern era communication has been drastically changed, most of it is digitized: we can use Facebook to keep in touch with old friends, far away loved ones and family; we have Twitter for microblogging, where we can express our opinions about politicians and famous people without being scared of repercussions; whatsapp is our most used instant messaging application for communicating to our loved ones, work and study colleagues; Instagram and Youtube are our favourite platforms for following material related to our hobbies and interests.

Using social media we have access to a lot of people and new ways for interaction, people share their experiences, support friends and get market informations. One problem of using social media is the common over-sharing of personal information which on a long run might cause negative consequences, such as sharing one’s location on a social platform or allowing messages between social networks without considering that these platforms are also used by criminals with malicious intentions. With all the benefits social media brought to us a huge number of risks also came along. A major concern within information security is the threat of social engineering attacks (Metalidou E. et al., 2014). The major risks in using these networks are: insufficient authentication controls, information leakage, injection flaws, information integrity, and more. The most frequently used cyber-attack methods are: Spear Phishing and Web Application Attacks. Spear Phishing is an attack attempt a criminal launches trying to persuade someone in opening an infected file or to click on a link, if the user of the program or email falls in this trap he might get attacked and share personal information without any awareness. There are several methods for mitigating social media security risks.

Many companies nowadays use a formal policy to guide their employees on the use of social media. These policies are guidelines for the users so that they know what is acceptable behaviour and what is unacceptable on social media, they might also guide the user through choosing an appropriate password and what information they can and cannot share. In many cases the only knowledge that a policy exists might not be enough to train an employee in a real life situation, for these situations there’s need for education, training and technology.

Humans are the weak link

The first step towards strengthening the weak link that is the human factor is education. Nowadays the internet is accessible to a large number of people, many children start using applications before they even go to school through gaming and online learning. Internet can be a very risky resource for children, reasons can be cyber-bullying, pedophilia and accessing adult websites. A report by IBM released on 2014 attributes some 95% of IT security breaches to human error. For these reasons education on how to use the internet and protect personal information should be teached in the earliest years of school though. During high school this education should be taken to the next level, teaching the youth more advanced concepts. The training can improve the online risks awareness in order to not commit actions that might lead to data and malware breaches. The training provided by companies should provide qualitative explanations about the acceptable use of social media and security policy, give examples of cyber-attacks and reporting incidents (Tayouri D., 2015).M

A new approach in training employees on the newest and advanced security procedures is the use of video games, because many other methods don’t require the users to think about the applied procedures. According to Ben-Asher et al. (2015), during a simplified Intrusion Detection System (IDS) simulation, responses from participants that have knowledge in cyber security indicated that they were able to distinguish between different types of cyber-attacks, whereas novice participants were not sensitive to the attack types.

The interactivity of a videogame can help the users to learn the concepts faster with a constant rate of improvement, the effectiveness of these training sessions should be assessed. A good assessment method could be the employment of drills, social engineering simulations on the employees to see if they detect the cyber-attack and if they report it to procedure (Tayouri D., 2015).

Technology

Another important aspect for mitigating the human error is technology, giving our best in this area means helping people to protect themselves better against cyber-attacks. In a social engineering scam it’s important to gather data about the the victim, therefore limiting unknown people from having information about them through privacy settings is crucial. Cyber-security is an accepted part of business life and organisations invest heavily in people, resources and budget (Green J., 2015).

Companies should ensure that employees always use devices with updated firewalls, antiviruses and anti-malware. It’s very important that employees know how to use protection software and scan regularly their devices, including flash-drives and downloaded material. A huge risk in using social media is fake identities, it’s hard to know who to trust when it comes from accepting friendship requests. Filtering friendship requests with the use of technology that checks the reliability of one’s profile and does cross-checks against other databases and users references can help by not letting user fall into malicious traps.

Threats can also be detected by using social media itself, if an employee seems to have a bad working relationship with it’s boss and company, if it appears to have a narcissistic behaviour or simply being a very frustrated person, this might lead the person to leak information that an be used to harm the company.

Conclusion

The way we communicate has been changed in the modern digital era. Social media became what we use to share our feelings and thoughts to the rest of the world, but it has a lot of security threats. To strengthen the human behaviour towards a more secure use of the internet and social media education must start in the earliest years of school and then continue during corporate employment. Sometimes uncommon methods towards education, such as interactive video-games can be more efficient than traditional education. Simulating cyber-attacks on the employees is an efficient way of establishing if the training was successful. Education by itself is not enough to ensure improvement, technology is needed. Enforcing privacy settings, using monitoring tools and using social media is an efficient way of protecting data. The combination of technology, training and education might be the best way to mitigate corporate information and reduce data leakage.
